The founder

40+ years in music law.

William Dunbar Murray has practiced music law for more than forty years. A former Recording Academy governor for the San Francisco Chapter, he has represented clients at every stage of their careers — from first-time songwriters to global stars such as Zendaya. He is also a working songwriter, artist and producer, and has built his own independent music business.

Music runs in the family. His son played in the Disneyland band for ten years and has appeared as a musician in several films, including Brad Pitt's Babylon and Harry Styles' Don't Worry Darling. Today his band, LaLa Brass, is the Breaks and Beats band for the LA Kings — a contract Muslaw Attorneys negotiated — and his daughter performs at the New York Renaissance Faire.

Over the years William has lived in the south of France, New York and Los Angeles, and represents the firm internationally at MIDEM, SXSW, Go North, Popkomm and the Sync-Summits.
